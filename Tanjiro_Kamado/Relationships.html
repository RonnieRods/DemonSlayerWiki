Quick Answers

Who is the only surviving family member of Tanjiro Kamado?
In the events of the story, Tanjiro has only one surviving family member, his younger sister Nezuko. In the modern times, his living descendants are Sumihiko and Kanata Kamado.
Does Tanjiro have any relationship with the Hashira?
Throughout most of the story, Tanjiro is involved in battles against demons while with the Hashira, and he comes to gain a personal relationship with them. While most of these relationships are positive, such as Giyu Tomioka's and Mitsuri Kanroji's close friendship with him, and gaining Gyomei Himejima's respect through his honest character, two Hashira remain resentful and distrustful of him, the two being Sanemi Shinazugawa and Obanai Iguro.
How does Tanjiro treat demons?
While he understands that demons are inherently tragic creatures who were once human, he acknowledges that their actions cannot be forgiven and they must be killed to atone for them. Early on, he shows respect towards certain demons who show signs of pain and personal suffering, such as Kyogai, Rui, and Akaza, but at other times, he comes to realize that some demons truly cannot be redeemed and remain true to their monstrous selves even as humans, such as the Swamp Demon, Hantengu, and Muzan Kibutsuji.

Relationships

Nezuko Kamado

Nezuko is the younger sister of Tanjiro and his only remaining family member alive after his family was killed while he was away selling charcoal. As such, Tanjiro, who is usually calm and polite, will become enraged and attack anybody who harms Nezuko. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 45 (Page 17). [1] He is extremely protective of her, though he doesn't stop her from helping him during battles that he cannot handle alone. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 12 (Page 5). [2] Being the sole surviving members of a poor family, the siblings have a very close bond, trusting and loving of one another. Tanjiro is likely the only one capable of actively calming Nezuko down by calling her name or singing to their mother's lullaby.


Even after Tanjiro is turned into a demon by Muzan and goes into a destructive rampage, Nezuko still approaches her brother and tries to hold him back despite the high possibility of herself being killed by him. Moreover, Tanjiro does not attack her even after tasting her blood, showing their love and familial bond still prevailed over his transformation and Muzan's desires for him to kill the Demon Slayers.


Takeo Kamado, Hanako Kamado, Shigeru Kamado, and Rokuta Kamado

Tanjiro was incredibly close to his other siblings. He would also often help them with their house chores and would cheer them up whenever they were upset. Even after their deaths, Tanjiro would hold them closely and dear to his heart; he even struggled to leave them behind in his dreams whilst he was under Enmu's Blood Demon Art. The deaths of his family would constantly remind Tanjiro of his single goal to defeat Muzan Kibutsuji, Tanjiro also used his family's death as a guiding light to destroy the demon too.


Tanjuro Kamado and Kie Kamado

Tanjuro and Kie are Tanjiro's parents, and just like Tanjiro's relationship with his other younger siblings, he had a close family bond with his parents. As a result, after both of his parents died, he used them as a guiding light to destroy Muzan Kibutsuji by using his father's Hinokami Kagura to fight Muzan and the other demons, as well as using his mother's flashback memory.


Giyu Tomioka

Tanjiro first meets Giyu after he tries to kill Nezuko, who had just been turned into a Demon. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 1 (Page 25). [3] After seeing his determination, Giyu instructs Tanjiro to go find the cultivator Sakonji Urokodaki, leading him onto the path of becoming a Demon Slayer. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 3 (Page 3). [4]


While distrustful of him at first, Tanjiro comes to highly respect Giyu later on, especially considering how both were students of Sakonji and users of the Water Breathing Style. Giyu himself grows to care enough for Tanjiro and Nezuko that he promises to commit seppuku (ritual suicide) alongside their master should Nezuko resort to eating humans. Tanjiro is the one who allows Giyu to open up and realize he needs to step up to as the inheritor of the Water Hashira and to not let his past losses and feeling of inadequacy prevent him from realizing this. In some ways, Tanjiro is his closest friend (aside from his late friend Sabito and, to some extent, fellow Hashira Shinobu Kocho.)


Zenitsu Agatsuma

At first, Tanjiro is annoyed with Zenitsu as their first encounter involved Zenitsu pestering a girl to marry him when she was clearly against it, forcing Tanjiro to remedy the situation by pulling Zenitsu away from the girl. Zenitsu was in denial that the girl didn't like him and immediately blamed Tanjiro for his failed proposal, causing Tanjiro to look at him in disgust. Despite this, Tanjiro understood that Zenitsu must have been strong enough to pass the Final Selection and that the two can rely on each other during battles against powerful Demons.


Tanjiro has also been shown to have taken up Zenitsu's fighting style. As can be seen when he recalled Zenitsu's words about Thunder Breathing, which allowed him to develop the sub-technique, Hinokami Kagura: Dancing Flash, to catch up to a fleeing Hantengu.


Though Tanjiro can be annoyed by Zenitsu's tantrums and womanizing behavior, he cares for Zenitsu as a close friend and is very patient with him. Zenitsu tends to be the one to inform Tanjiro of the city life and occasionally explain things due to Tanjiro's naivety. He cares for Tanjiro as a close friend and admires his strength and kindness. Zenitsu also attempts to get Tanjiro to acknowledge the prospect of being a future brother-in-law, considering Zenitsu's crush on Nezuko.


Inosuke Hashibira

Initially, despite both being Demon Slayers and being in a tight situation to kill a Demon, Inosuke is instead amazed by Tanjiro's strength and tries to fight him, not caring about the bigger picture. Even after the Demon is defeated, Inosuke would not let up. Tanjiro is occasionally annoyed with his insistence on a fight but is extremely patient with him. Ironically, Tanjiro's naivety and kindness allow him to unwittingly manipulate Inosuke or even annoy him. Despite this, Tanjiro manages to be a positive influence on Inosuke as he convinces Inosuke to start using his head and think during battle. The two tend to also both be naive about city life. Ultimately, they get along as good friends and close allies.


Later on as the series progressed, Inosuke and Tanjiro's friendship was shown to have become incredibly strong and close to the point that the both of them admitted that they now both see and consider each other as brothers and both made a promised that if anyone of them began to stray from the right path, the other would do their best to correct and help them.


Upon Tanjiro's transformation into a Demon, Inosuke was one of the first demon slayers to try and kill him before he could eat someone and prevent him from going a dark path. However as Inosuke was about to cut him, he remembered all of Tanjiro's kindness and how he sees him as a brother, causing him to then break down in tears and stop his attack during the battle due to not being able to bring himself to kill him.


Sakonji Urokodaki

When they first met, Sakonji initially hesitated in training Tanjiro after noting his inability and reluctance to kill. He eventually relented and taught Tanjiro for over a year in Water Breathing. After his past 13 students were killed in Final Selection, Sakonji would only allow him to enter if he managed to cut through a very large boulder, as a way to protect him. Eventually, Tanjiro succeeded in slicing it, entered and survived Final Selection, even managing to defeat an old foe of Sakonji. Seeing his latest student survive after so many failures haunting him, Sakonji tearfully hugged Tanjiro and congratulated him on coming home.


Tanjiro was shocked to learn that both Sakonji and Giyu had taken an oath to kill themselves if Nezuko had ever harmed a person.


Shinobu Kocho

Shinobu helps train Tanjiro during his rehabilitation period, and, while she initially does not understand his drive to protect his Demon sister, she does grow to care for him. Though she trained Tanjiro, Zenitsu, and Inosuke, she seems to be the closest (or, is at least the least irritated) with Tanjiro because unlike Zenitsu and Inosuke, he took his training seriously from the start. Shinobu also recommends Tanjiro to Oyakata-Sama for the Infinity Train mission, stating that Tanjiro "has a pure soul and is capable of striving hard on a daily basis". During a conversation with him, she even divulges her past where her sister was killed by a powerful Upper Rank.


Kanao Tsuyuri

When Kanao first saw Tanjiro at the Final Selection, they did not have any sort of interaction as she remained aloof and quiet at the end of the ceremony. Later on in the series, when they meet again on Mount Natagumo, Kanao gets into a physical altercation with Tanjiro as he tries to protect his sister from getting killed and as result gets knocked out cold by Kanao. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 44 (Pages 10-12). [5]


Tanjiro is brought to the Butterfly Mansion to recover and sees her in the garden, he immediately recognizes her as one of the survivors from the Final Selection but fails to recognize her as his assailant. Later on when they begin to train, Tanjiro struggles to beat Kanao in all of the tasks due to her advanced abilities but he is able to adapt and beat her. During one of the games, the task is to splash the opponent with a strong smelling tea however instead of dumping it on Kanao, Tanjiro instead places the cup on her head to avoid splashing her. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 50 (Pages 20-21). [6] Tanjiro gets curious as to why Kanao needs to flip a coin to make decisions. He decides to flip her coin to encourage her to be optimistic and for her to open her heart. As he flashes her a smile as he bids her farewell. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 53 (Pages 14-20). [7]


As the story progresses, Kanao starts showing more emotion and decisiveness, especially when Tanjiro is brought back to recover at the Butterfly Mansion following the battles in the Entertainment District. Upon discovering that he had awaken, she drops the flower vase on the floor and tears up while telling him she's glad he had awoken. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 99 (Pages 20-21). [8] When the other inhabitants of the Mansion begin to bicker while visiting Tanjiro, she tries to get everyone to quiet down but ultimately ends up yelling at everyone after he falls asleep again. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 100 (Page 10). [9]


During the battle against Muzan Kibutsuji, Kanao is left as the only conscious Demon Slayer left and tries to will herself to continue fighting. As Muzan is about to deliver the final blow, Tanjiro momentarily immobilizes Muzan and manages to carry Kanao to safety. Realizing that he has awaken, Kanao sheds tears as she repeatedly calls out his name and he responds to her by apologizing for being late. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 191 (Pages 17-19). [10]


After Tanjiro turns into a Demon, Kanao cries when seeing Nezuko trying to calm her brother. Her sympathy for the siblings leads to her once again using Flower Breathing Final Form: Equinoctial Vermillion Eye and create an opening to inject the Demon-to-human cure into Tanjiro, putting her own safety at risk and showing how much she cares for them.


After Tanjiro successfully returns to his human form, she cries of happiness and is visibly relieved to see him safely back as a human, the two exchanging tearful smiles as they lie across from each other.


Tanjiro and Kanao would eventually marry and start a family, having two great-grandchildren by the names of Kanata Kamado and Sumihiko Kamado between them.


Genya Shinazugawa

When they first interacted, Tanjiro in a warning gesture gripped Genya's arm to the point where it could have broken as punishment for roughly handling Kanata Ubuyashiki right after the final selection. They reunited again in the butterfly mansion right after Tanjiro had recovered his injuries during his battle with Lower Rank 5 but when Tanjiro attempted to greet him, Genya coldly ignored him.


They crossed paths again in the Swordsmith Village when Tanjiro spotted Genya in the hot springs, and tried interacting with him, to make an effort to get along with him. Unfortunately, Genya was getting annoyed and creeped out by Tanjiro. However, they soon formed a deep bond when they first fought together. Not too long after, Genya came to warm up to Tanjiro and they became close friends.


Tanjiro also tried to help mend Genya's relationship with Sanemi, and was quick to defend him when Sanemi nearly gouged his eyes out. Genya is very touched at Tanjiro's defense of him, even admitting that Hantengu couldn't have been defeated without him. Genya later taught Tanjiro how to do repetitive actions during Gyomei's Hashira Training, and Tanjiro reveals to Sanemi that he doesn't sense any malice from his older brother, saying that Sanemi still dearly loves him. Later Tanjiro cries when he hears of Genya's passing.


They soon formed some sort of a bond when they first fought together. Not too long after, Genya came to warm up to Tanjiro and they became close friends. Genya even told Tanjiro about Repetitive Action, and how to perform it. After Genya's death at the end of the battle against Upper Rank One, Tanjiro was deeply saddened and cried tears for his fallen friend, vowing to end Muzan once and for all.


Kyojuro Rengoku

At first, Kyojuro greatly disapproved of Tanjiro for traveling with Nezuko, a Demon. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 45 (Page 4). [11] However, during their joint mission to defeat Enmu, they formed a bond and became close despite only spending a few hours together. This bond even promoted Kyojuro to offer taking Tanjiro as a Tsuguko. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 54 (Page 7). [12]


Kyojuro shared his final moments with Tanjiro and deeply inspired him with his words of encouragement, entrusting the boy to pass on his final words to his younger brother and father and expressing his acceptance of Nezuko as a true member of the Corps. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 66 (Pages 3-4). [13] After the Flame Hashira's passing, Tanjiro continued to hold his deep respect and admiration for him, becoming infuriated whenever others would speak ill about him. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 148 (Pages 16-17). [14] During his fight against Daki, Tanjiro remembers Kyojuro's words which motivate him throughout his battle. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 77 (Page 14). [15]


During the Final Battle Arc, Tanjiro uses Kyojuro's handguard for his sword in battle. Tanjiro also recalls Kyojuro's words often during the arc, once when he was trying to turn his blade bright red, and once before overwhelming Muzan with the Thirteenth Form of Sun Breathing.


Tengen Uzui

Tanjiro first met Tengen at his trial alongside the other Hashira. Upon hearing that he had been travelling with a Demon, Tengen initially vouched to kill him for disobeying the rules and rejected Kagaya's blessing to allow Nezuko and Tanjiro to travel together. When it was revealed that Tanjiro came face-to-face with Muzan Kibutsuji, Tengen, along with the others, were shocked as no Hashira had ever done so before. Tengen then began questioning him about Muzan's appearance, abilities, and whereabouts.


The two met again at the Butterfly Mansion, where Tengen attempted to kidnap Aoi and Naho for a mission. Tanjiro became infuriated at his actions, and stated that he does not acknowledge Tengen's superior position as a Hashira. Tengen in response, exploded at him for his disrespectful attitude. Their exchange eventually cools down when Tanjiro, along with Inosuke and Zenitsu, offer to accompany Tengen on his mission in place of the girls.


As they formally began their mission, Tanjiro's na√Øvety pleases Tengen, seemingly melting away any tension between them. Several days after infiltrating the Entertainment District, Tengen met up with both Tanjiro and Inosuke, suggesting that they leave the mission for their own benefit due to their lower rankings. Claiming that he felt bad for putting them through with it since their possible encounter with an Upper Rank could lead to their deaths.


When Tengen arrived to the battle against Daki, he scolded Tanjiro for his lack of control over Nezuko even after receiving a blessing from the Master. Yet rather than attacking her himself, he suggested Tanjiro put her to sleep with a lullaby. With Inosuke and Zenitsu's arrival to the fight, along with Gyutaro's appearance, Tengen openly claimed to the Demon Siblings that all 3 of the boys were his brilliant Tsuguko, all with relentless grit, and that they would all together defeat the Demons. Tengen's speech moved Tanjiro, and reminded him of Kyojuro Rengoku.


When Gyutaro attempted to kill Hinatsuru, who had been providing support from the side by firing Kunai, Tanjiro quickly came to her rescue through a combined Water Breathing and Hinokami Kagura technique. Tengen then arrived and gave Tanjiro his gratitude before fighting off Gyutaro. As the two worked together to take down Gyutaro, Tanjiro became increasingly concerned over Tengen's deteriorating health due to the poison and constantly tried to protect him from oncoming attacks. Even during situations when Tanjiro is risking his life, Tengen consistently managed to throw Tanjiro out of the way, or protect him before he can be seriously wounded.


In the final stretch, Tengen, despite having lost one hand and heavily poisoned, came to Tanjiro's aid to deflect a fatal strike from Gyutaro. Tengen held Gyutaro off, losing his left eye in the process, much to Tanjiro's shock. Yet his actions provided Tanjiro with an opportunity to behead the demon, which Tengen alerted him to act upon quickly. At the end of the battle, Tengen turned around to warn Tanjiro of Gyutaro's oncoming attack instead of fleeing, showing his concern for him.


Following the completion of their mission, Tengen finally acknowledges Tanjiro's abilities by telling Obanai that he "has grown to become strong enough" to fill his vacancy as he retired.


After the final battle against Muzan, Tengen and his wives came to visit Tanjiro in the medical ward.


Muichiro Tokito

During their first few encounters, Tanjiro and Muichiro held a strained relationship. Muichiro reprimanded Tanjiro by flicking a stone at his head after the latter interrupted Kagaya Ubuyashiki's speech, and Tanjiro was bewildered by Muichiro's rude treatment towards Kotetsu at the Swordsmith Village. However, after hearing Tanjiro's advice about helping others, Muichiro was influenced enough to alter his mindset, saving Kotetsu and Kozo Kanamori during Gyokko's attack on the village. Tanjiro's advice, along with his appearance, reminded Muichiro of his late father, which further allowed him to regain the memories of his early life. Muichiro thanked Tanjiro for such, despite the former being oblivious to his influence.


During the battle against Hantengu, Muichiro called out to Tanjiro from afar, and threw him his unfinished sword from Haganezuka, telling him to use it and finish off the Demon. Tanjiro later thanked him for giving him the weapon.


After subsequently regaining his memories, Muichiro and Tanjiro became good friends, with Muichiro showing heavy bias towards Tanjiro when they trained together during the Hashira training session. After hearing about Muichiro's death at the hands of Kokushibo, Tanjiro shed tears over the loss of his friend and vowed to himself he would continue fighting hard.


Tamayo

Tanjiro meets Tamayo in the first mission in Asakusa Arc, and Tamayo has helped him to know more about Muzan and even suggests ways to turn Nezuko into a human. Throughout Tanjiro's journey, Tamayo investigates and tries hard to produce medicines in order to make demons human again. Tanjiro also shows respect to Tamayo and collects the Twelve Kizuki's blood to speed up her progress.


Yushiro

Tanjiro meets Yushiro in the first mission in Asakusa Arc, and it seems like Yushiro doesn't like Tanjiro because he doesn't like that another person is squeezing into his life with Tamayo. Despite this, while in the Infinity Castle, Yushiro keeps on supporting and wishes for Tanjiro to stay alive. And when the battle's over, Yushiro visits Tanjiro and praises him.


Gyomei Himejima

At the first Hashira Meeting, Gyomei judges that Tanjiro must be killed as he is a Demon Slayer traveling alongside a Demon. Later, during the Hashira Training Arc, Tanjiro shows Gyomei his determination, integrity, and hard work, which leads to him being accepted by the Stone Hashira.


Mitsuri Kanroji

Ever since the Hashira meeting with both the Kamado siblings, Mitsuri has expressed empathy and kindness towards Tanjiro and his sister. The two first interact during the Swordsmith Village Arc, where Mitsuri ran down from the hotsprings to meet him. As she ran, her breasts began pouring out of her yukata, and Tanjiro warned her before quickly fixing it as she tackled him. Mitsuri then cried to Tanjiro, telling him that someone at the hot spring ignored her when she said hello. As she cried, Tanjiro notified her that dinner would be ready soon, and it was matsutake rice. Hearing this made Mitsuri excited.


Later, Tanjiro talked with Mitsuri over a big meal. Tanjiro complimented how much she was able to eat, to which she replied that she really only "nibbled". Tanjiro then told her that the guy who ignored her was Genya Shinazugawa, to which Mitsuri was confused, as she tells him the Wind Hashira, Sanemi Shinazugawa claimed to have no siblings. Seeing how Genya still hadn't joined them for dinner, they decide to take a walk to deliver rice balls to him. As they walk together, Mitsuri confesses her motive for joining the Demon Slayer Corps, much to her embarrassment. Mitsuri then compliments how Tanjiro had managed to become far stronger than he was following his defeat of an upper rank.


Tanjiro then gave a statement of motivation that he will work even harder for the sake of defeating Muzan Kibutsuji, charming Mitsuri and a nearby Kakushi. Hearing this, the Love Hashira informed Tanjiro of a secret weapon that she hopes will help Tanjiro become stronger before parting ways with the Kamado siblings. During the battle with Hantengu alongside his clones, Misturi saved Tanjiro from being crushed by Zohakuten's wooden dragon.


Before Mitsuri could decapitate Zohakuten, Tanjiro informed her that her attack would not work due to him being a clone, much to her shock. This inadventently caused her to get hit by Zohakuten's deafening sound attack, temporarily stunning her. Tanjiro, Genya, and Nezuko all rushed over to save her from Zohakuten, before attempting to carry her to safety. Tanjiro ordered them to protect Mitsuri, saying she has the most potential out of all of them, and therefore she is their one ray of hope.


Moved by his words, Mitsuri quickly regained her strength and used her sword to slash through a group of incoming lighning attacks from Zohakuten, amazing Tanjiro and Nezuko. Mitsuri cried, thanking them for saving her, while apologizing for messing up as a Hashira, stating that she would never let any of her friends die. Getting back on her feet, Mitsuri told them to leave Zohakuten to her, and go after Hantengu's main body. Following Hantengu's defeat, Mitsuri ran to the group and gave Tanjiro and Nezuko a hug.


Sanemi Shinazugawa

Tanjiro has had a disdain for the Wind Hashira ever since he brutally stabbed Nezuko multiple times in an attempt to goad her into attacking. Despite this, Tanjiro shows emotional restraint when heading to Hashira Training, expressing his excitement even though he proclaims that he doesn't respect Sanemi to his face. After the final battle against Muzan, Tanjiro's dislike for Sanemi seems to have faded away, as he is eager to go see him and greet him while conversing with Kanao. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 204 (Page 19). [16]


Muzan Kibutsuji

Tanjiro harbors a deep hatred for Muzan as he recognizes him as not only the leader and creator of the Demons but also as the perpetrator behind the slaughter of his family using his scent. He yells at the Demon as he casually escapes that he will follow him to the ends of the Earth and that Muzan will die by his blade.


Akaza

When the two first met, Akaza immediately attempted to kill Tanjiro upon seeing him on the ground, quickly rejecting him as weak and focusing his attention on Kyojuro. After Kyojuro is fatally wounded, Akaza is force to flee from the breaking sun but was confronted and speared from the back by Tanjiro, who angrily called him a coward for fleeing the battle. Akaza became infuriated and this hatred only grew stronger when Muzan expressed his disappointment in his inability to kill Tanjiro.


In the Infinity Castle, Akaza tracked Tanjiro down and engaged in a fierce battle with the Demon Slayer and Giyu. During this fight Akaza praised Tanjiro for his newfound strength and apologizes for dismissing him as a weakling during his fight with Kyojuro. He ends up claiming that he was glad that Kyojuro died because of his foolish mindset and his unlikeliness to get stronger, causing Tanjiro to be enraged at the comment. When Akaza claimed that he was actually praising Kyojuro, Tanjiro retorted that he instead was degrading him, like he did everyone.


Akaza responds that he in fact only hated weaklings, and began to explain his Social Darwinist philosophy. Tanjiro revoked his claims by stating that strong had to protect the weak, and uses caring for infants as an example, before vowing to kill the demon so he couldn't get away with doing what he wanted any longer. This reminded Akaza of his old mentor, confusing and angering him even more. After some more fighting, Tanjiro awakened both the Transparent World and the Selfless State, and after calling Akaza out by name, he swiftly decapitated him.


However, Akaza, filled with a will to not die, kept on moving and began to regenerate his head, much to Tanjiro's shock. However, after remembering his painful human past, Akaza came to realize he truly wanted to kill himself. He smiled slightly and was noticed by Tanjiro, who also detected a scent of gratitude from his body. In the aftermath of attacking himself, he accepted that the Demon Slayer had defeated him in a fair battle, and Tanjiro eventually witnesses Akaza's body finally crumble to ash. Although confused, Tanjiro likely smelled Akaza's regret and emotional turmoil, and let out an understanding "ah..".


Hantengu

Tanjiro shows Hantengu no mercy, knowing that Hantengu was a mass murderer who completely refused to own up to his actions and was evil long before becoming a demon, much to the ire of both the manifestations of his hatred (Zohakuten) and resentment (Urami).


Daki

As the main threat in Yoshiwara and one of the Demon Slayers sent to kill her, Daki and Tanjiro were natural enemies from the start. Daki's act of kidnapping Koinatsu and her attempts to kill him only fuelled their animosity, peaking when Tanjiro became enraged by Daki destroying an entire street of the Entertainment District and killing numerous innocent people in scorn. However, Tanjiro comforted Daki and her brother in their deaths and told them that all the two siblings have is each other, inspiring Daki to make up with Gyutaro before their ultimate fates.
