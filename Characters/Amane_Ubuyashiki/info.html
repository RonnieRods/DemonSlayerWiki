Amane Ubuyashiki (産うぶ屋や敷しき あまね Ubuyashiki Amane), née Amane Himorogi (神ひも籬ろぎ あまね Himorogi Amane) Kimetsu no Yaiba Second Fanbook. [2] was a supporting character in Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba. She was the wife and primary caretaker of Kagaya Ubuyashiki and the mother of Hinaki, Nichika, Kiriya, Kanata and Kuina Ubuyashiki.

Appearance

Amane was a woman of average height and pale skin complexion. She possessed large, plum purple eyes (with pink eyeshadow) and short eyebrows, a trait seen in her children. She wore her white hair down and swept to both the left and right at the front and in a short ponytail in the back, with two strands of hair loose at the side of her head. Her beauty was apparent as Muichiro Tokito once described her as appearing like a forest spirit when they first met, and Mitsuri Kanroji, being asked to speak by Amane, took the chance to admire her beauty.


Amane wore a salmon-colored kimono with an ombre to orange at the top with flower patterns with a dark-indigo haori with numerous blue butterfly-patterns. She also wore a brown with a paler shade striped-obi with an orange obiage supporting it.


Personality

Amane was shown to possess the same quiet, strict and serious disposition as her husband and children, acting professionally and tactfully when communicating with the Hashira during her time filling in for her husband when his health began to fail. She was shown to be just as devoted to the Demon Slayer Corps as her husband and children as well, and spared no time in trying to figure out anything that could be used to turn the tide of battle to their favor.


As well as this, Amane has been shown to truly love and care for her husband, Kagaya Ubuyashiki, and is seen constantly nursing him and staying by his side as his body slowly began to fail and his health further worsened. In fact, the only time Amane dropped her usual controlled demeanor was when Kagaya's health began to severely plummet and he began to cough out blood, causing her to immediately panic and worrt as she barked out orders for her children to help her treat him, showing a genuine sense of love between them. During a moment alone with him, Kagaya held her hand and thanked her for everything she had done, causing her to quietly cry and return his sentiment.


Later, upon Kagaya reaching the end of his life and being approached by Muzan, Amane chose to stay by his side and commit suicide via an explosive trap in order to die together with him and severely injure Muzan at the same time, fully showcasing her immense devotion to both her husband and the Demon Slayer Corps. Amane was also noted to have been a loving, caring and strict parent to her children, making sure to raise them to be independent and emotionally strong in order to prepare them for her and her husband's inevitable deaths. She showed a strong amount of faith and love towards her children and their potentials.



Abilities

Overall Abilities

Leadership Skills: When her husband's health begins to fall, Amane shows herself to be a highly competent leader and military commander, having been able to fill in for her husband in his duties as Corps Commander for the Demon Slayer Corps during the final days of his life. This is seen when Amane possesses the necessary skills to direct and lead the Hashira themselves despite their extreme personalities and dispositions.


Medical Skills: Amane has also demonstrated to be highly skilled in medicine, being married to Kagaya as his nurse and caregiver. This is seen where she is the one primarily in charge of treating her husband's illness and caring for his ailing body from his ancestral curse. Amane later further showcased her medical skills when she managed to treat and successfully saved a near death Muichiro Tokito after he was severely injured fighting and killing his first demon prior to joining the Demon Slayer Corps.


Trivia

  • Amane's given name is completely comprised of hiragana characters, thus having no semantic properties. However, if her Mandarin name is taken into consideration, her name contains the Kanji/Hànzì for "heaven" (産うぶ ama), and "sound" (音ね ne).
    • Amane's maiden name "Himorogi" (神ひも籬ろぎ lit. "divine fence") refers to sacred altars in Shintō belief for worship towards the Kami.
  • Amane came from a family of Shintō Priests. She was born with white hair, and for the sake of her children and husband's health, she performed purification rituals even on snowy days, never missing a single one. Kimetsu no Yaiba Second Fanbook. [2]
  • Amane is older than her husband by 4 years. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Volume 16. [3]
  • When Amane gave birth to quintuplets. it amazed both the midwife and doctor. Kimetsu no Yaiba Second Fanbook. [2]
  • Amane shares her English voice actress with Ruka Rengoku.
