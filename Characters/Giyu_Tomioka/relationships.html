Quick Answers

What is the relationship between Tanjiro and Giyu?
Giyu attempts to protect Tanjiro from his demonized sister, Nezuko, and is taken aback when Tanjiro defends her. Recognizing Tanjiro's potential after a close encounter, Giyu refers him to his master, Sakonji Urokodaki, for training. Despite initial hostility, Giyu grows to deeply care for the siblings, risking his life and breaching the Demon Slayers' code to safeguard them.
Who does Giyu Tomioka have romantic feelings for?
There is no explicit evidence within the manga and anime suggesting that Giyu Tomioka harbors romantic feelings for any character.
Does Giyu have a love interest in Shinobu?
There is no clear evidence of a romantic relationship between them. Giyu's relationship with Shinobu appears to be strictly professional and platonic.
Why does Obanai have a dislike for Giyu?
Obanai dislikes Giyu because of his taciturn personality and refusal to explain his motives and actions, which, along with Sanemi Shinazugawa, Obanai interprets as him possessing a superiority complex. Despite Giyu's want to express himself, his quiet personality and inferiority complex about being a Hashira prevents him, which further worsens how he is seen by Obanai.

Relationships

Tanjiro Kamado

Giyu tries to save Tanjiro from Nezuko Kamado, who had been turned into a Demon, but is shocked to see Tanjiro trying to protect her. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 1 (Page 25). [1] After nearly getting taken down by Tanjiro, he sense his potential and sends him to his master, Sakonji Urokodaki, so he can train him to become a Demon Slayer. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 1 (Page 53). [2]


While initially hostile towards them, Giyu has come to care a lot about the siblings and willing to violate the Demon Hunters' code of conduct by putting his life on the line in order to protect them. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 44 (Page 7). [3] Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 46 (Page 10). [4]


While Tanjiro was struggling against Rui, Giyu saved him and killed Rui with his Eleventh Form, Dead Calm.


Tanjiro is the one who allows Giyu to open up and realize he needs to step up to being the inheritor of the Water Breathing Style, and not let his past losses and feeling of inadequacy prevent him from realizing this. The two actually have more in common than they think, thinking on similar wavelengths. In some ways, Tanjiro is his closest friend aside from his late friend Sabito, and to some extent Shinobu Kocho.


He is shown to care a lot for Tanjiro, which is further seen when he becomes greatly angered when Akaza attempts to kill Tanjiro, losing his usual stoic demeanor and jumping in to protect him from the Upper Rank. When Tanjiro is presumed dead after the battle with Muzan Kibutsuji, Giyu cries and blames himself for not being able to protect him. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 201. [5] When Tanjiro becomes a Demon, however, Giyu shows little hesitation to attack him with the intent to kill unlike others who are close to Tanjiro. He does hesitate to kill Tanjiro when he notices Tanjiro is fighting Muzan's control.


Nezuko Kamado

When the two first met, Giyu captured and attempted to kill Nezuko, however he let her and Tanjiro go when he saw Nezuko's determination to protect her brother.


Later on, when he saved Tanjiro from Rui, he blocked Shinobu's sword from striking the girl and told Tanjiro to flee while he holds her off. At the Hashira Meeting, Giyu went as far as vowing to commit seppuku if Nezuko ever attacked a human, showing how much he cared and felt responsible for her.


While the demon slayers were battling Muzan, Giyu's haori was shredded after the fight. While Giyu was in a coma from his wounds, Nezuko restored most of the haori thanks to her sewing skills. Giyu was very touched and sent Nezuko lots of gifts such as hairpins, western kimonos, and different accessories which made Zenitsu jealous.


Hashira


Due to his Imposter Syndrome, Giyu views himself as beneath the "real" Hashira and not worth standing alongside them. For this reason, he initially refuses to partake in Hashira Training, much to the dismay of the other Hashira. In addition, his personality made him emotionally distant towards the other Hashira.


Shinobu Kocho

Giyu and Shinobu are both Hashira of the Demon Slayer Corps. They have opposing outward personalities and initially seem to be at odds with each other, with Giyu being shocked when she suggested he is disliked. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 44 (Page 17). [6] Despite this, he respects her work ethic and acknowledges her dedication as a Hashira. Giyu always answers constructive questions about the mission or work. From his perspective, Giyu mentions that Shinobu talks to him a lot while also noting that she is a diligent and serious person. For her part, Shinobu thinks that Giyu should talk to people more. Kimetsu no Yaiba First Fanbook. [7]


Both also come to care about the Kamado siblings and are visibly upset by the hostile treatment of the other Hashira towards Tanjiro and Nezuko. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 45 (Page 15). [8] When Giyu later hears of Shinobu's death, he expresses shock and recalls her smiling face. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 193. [9] Shinobu also internally expressed support and worked with the other Hashira as they attempted to bond with Giyu on one occasion. Kimetsu no Yaiba: One-Winged Butterfly. [10]


Sanemi Shinazugawa

Sanemi and Giyu seem to not have a very good relationship as their personalities are quite different. Sanemi with his usually loud and abrasive speech while Giyu is kept calm and quiet. The only time there is respect shown between the two is when Kagaya is present. In addition, Sanemi believed Giyu's aloof personality and claim that he "wasn't like the rest of the Hashira" to be a result of his supposed arrogance, not helped by Giyu never clarifying his emotional turmoil. Like all Hashira, Giyu considered Sanemi to be above him in terms of worthiness of being a Hashira, but he did give a sarcastic jab at Sanemi's expense when he griped that awakening a Demon Slayer Mark was "easy". It is shown that Giyu attempts to have a friendly gesture towards Sanemi when Tanjiro offers to eat ohagi together, and considered carrying ohagi in the hopes to befriend him.


In the final battle, both Hashira put aside all differences to fight Muzan Kibutsuji. In one instance, they manage to attain a Bright Red Nichirin Sword by making friction between their own blades. By the end, they are the only active Hashira to have survived the final battle. After the Demon Slayers are disbanded, the two Hashira look to each other and smile, since their long journey finally ended. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 204 (Page 11). [11] A photo depicts them awkwardly eating together.


Sakonji Urokodaki

As the former Water Hashira, Sakonji is Giyu's master and the one who taught him the Water Breathing Style as the cultivator. He holds him on high regard and Sakonji seems to have a lot of trust on his former student as well, as seen when he accepts his judgement of Tanjiro and Nezuko's circumstances by agreeing to train and shelter them. Kimetsu no Yaiba Manga: Chapter 3 (Page 2). [12]


Sabito

While Sabito is long dead before the series started, it was revealed that both he and Sabito were good friends as they went through similar situations of losing their families to Demons and bonded well as a result. Later, during the Final Selection Exam, Sabito died at the hands of the Hand Demon trying to save his life and others who participated in the exam. Sabito's death caused Giyu to feel so much guilt to the point where he doubted his own abilities and considered abandoning his position as the Water Hashira.





Murata

Murata was apart of the same Final Selection Exam as Giyu and Sabito. After Giyu is injured by a demon, Sabito entrusted Giyu into the care of Murata.


During the final battle against Muzan, Giyu gave an order to Murata explicitly by calling his name. Murata was touched that Giyu remembered his name despite not interacting with him much.


Tsutako Tomioka

Tsutako is Giyu's older sister. Not much is known about Giyu and Tsutako's relationship, but both used to have a strong and loving sibling relationship. One night, Tsutako hid the young Giyu and sacrificed herself to protect him from Demons attacking their home. Giyu also cared a lot for his sister that he feels guilty for her death and thought he is better off dead before Sabito snapped at him because of that.


Mitsuri Kanroji

Giyu and Mitsuri don't share a relationship, However, she occasionally calls him adorable. Giyu thinks shows too much skin. Kimetsu no Yaiba Second Fanbook. [13]


Obanai Iguro

Obanai hated Giyu, apparently due to disliking the Water Hashira's stoic "rat face". For his part, Giyu was saddened that Obanai disliked him. Later in their fight against Muzan, the two were shown protecting each other and working together.
